About Me


Hi, I am a content creator, developer, and writer.


I run a small variety gaming YouTube channel that focuses on traditional-style content and minimal editing; think something you would see in 2015. I primarily do gameplay videos, such as reviews and tutorials, but I may also do other kinds of content occasionally as well. Oh, and I absolutely despise drama content; it's lazy, it's uninspired, it's cheap, and you will never catch me doing it. Anyway, if that is something that resonates with you, I'd highly appreciate a humble subscription.


Ever since I acquired my first personal computer, I've been fascinated by coding and general development. I first started with game development around 2018 in Roblox Studio, then I transitioned to Unity and Unreal Engine. Sometime in late 2019, I started experimenting with HTML and CSS, and soon fell in love with it, so I've been doing it ever since. In early 2026, I came across an issue where whenever I would be working on something, I would need to have 10 browser tabs open at once just to have access to extremely useful tools, such as converters and a color picker. Either that, or I would need to download some sketchy and outdated software to do the same. So I set forth to make an all-in-one desktop app that came with pretty much everything you could ever need, and I called it A.P.M (short for "Applicatio Productivitatis Multiusus" = "Multipurpose Productivity Application"). When A.P.M was finally finished, I decided to make a return to the game development industry and started work on a 2D military strategy game called 1791, which is my debut game.


By late 2025, I started work on my first book, a novel, to be precise. It follows young Henry Smith as he joins the Union Army to fight in the American Civil War. After the war, he returns home just to find it completely abandoned with no traces of where his mother and little brother went. The continuation of the story sees him finally reuniting with his lost family after years of searching and all the events that come after.
